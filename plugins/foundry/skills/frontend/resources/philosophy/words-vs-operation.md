# Words-style vs. Operation-style

Every element family answers in two registers. Choose per task and per customer; many screens mix both.

## Words-style
Forms and text: the customer reads labels and **types, tabs, types, tabs**. The fastest path to making a content-item's structure operational. Elements: lookups, inline edits, conditional groups. Optimise tab order and keyboard flow (see `layout-and-flow.md`).

## Operation-style
Instruments and readouts: gauges, progress bars, badges, pills, tags, charts, levers, stat readouts. The customer **glances and acts** rather than reads-and-types. This is the language of dashboards and reporting UI. Elements: pill/tag/badge, gauge/progress, readout/stat, cover tiles.

## Choosing
- Capturing structured data quickly → words-style.
- Monitoring, comparing, deciding at a glance → operation-style.
- A writer's dashboard is mostly operation-style; their metadata editor is words-style. Same customer, both registers.

Record the chosen register in the marker's `style` field so later agents keep the screen coherent.
