# Rich-Data Presentation Techniques

Principles for screens that carry a lot of information without overwhelming the customer.

## Information scent
Every label, pill, and affordance should make its outcome predictable. Strong scent lets the customer find and act without reading everything. (This is why lookups and tags beat free text — recognition over recall.)

## Progressive disclosure
Show what's needed now; reveal the rest on demand (conditional-field-groups, expandable detail, drill-in from dashboards). Protects working memory.

## Structural honesty
Let the UI's shape mirror the data's shape: an array-of-references becomes a row of pills; a bounded quantity becomes a gauge; a record becomes a card. The customer learns the model by using the UI.

## Recognition over recall
Prefer choosing from shown options to remembering and typing. Constrains data quality *and* lowers load (Hick's Law trade-off: group large option sets).

## Glance-vs-read
Decide, per element, whether the customer should *glance* (operation-style: gauges, badges, readouts) or *read-and-type* (words-style). Don't make someone read a paragraph to learn a number, or squint at a gauge to edit a title.

## Density as a dial, not a dogma
Rich does not mean cramped. Offer the density toggle; default moderate; keep contrast and target sizes constant.
