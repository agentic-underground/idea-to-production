# Interaction Paradigms

Pick the paradigm from **customer + device + task**, then express it through elements and layout.

## Form-as-conversation
Sequential, guided, one decision at a time (wizard-like). Good for occasional users, complex branching, or phone (tap, advance). Pairs with conditional-field-groups and a clear next-affordance.

## Form-as-document
All fields visible at once, reference-able, customer controls the order. Good for power users and review/edit of a known record on a large screen. Pairs with dense-tolerant layouts and strong tab order.

## Spreadsheet-dense
Grid of editable cells; maximal density; keyboard-first. Good for high-volume back-office work. (Large grids → see `../ROADMAP.md` for virtualization.)

## Dashboard-explorative
Aggregated, operation-style views; glance, compare, drill in. Good for reporting and monitoring. Pairs with gauges, readouts, browse grids.

## On the book example
Seller (catalogue) leans form-as-document + dashboard; reader (library) is dashboard-explorative + delight; writer (composition) mixes a focused conversation for setup with an operation-style dashboard for progress and revenue. Same data, three paradigms — which is why eliciting the customer comes first.
