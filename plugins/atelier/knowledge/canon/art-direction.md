# Art direction — composition · light · colour · narrative · medium · the award bar

> The aesthetic-taste layer: how a *generative image* (or any pictorial figure) earns the word
> **award-winning** rather than merely *generated*. The sibling of
> [`visual-foundations.md`](visual-foundations.md) (which judges the *screen*); this judges the *image*.
> Most "it looks AI / flat / generic" reactions are a **named violation** here — cite the name and a
> **named exemplar** so the fix is teachable, not a vibe.
>
> **How to wield it:** an image reviewer is heavyweight because it is *grounded*. For every finding name
> **(a)** the principle, **(b)** the violation, **(c)** an exemplar that does it right. A finding-free
> pass is *earned*, never assumed — and the [artifact floor](#7-the-artifact-floor) caps the score
> before any of this taste matters. The exemplars cited below are drawn from the marketplace's
> award-corpus research (a curated set of 120 best-in-class works across genres · styles · scenes ·
> landscapes · diagrams · action, each tagged with the principle it teaches).

## 1. Composition — where the marks sit

> *The eye is led on purpose, or it wanders. Award-tier framing is inevitable; entry-level is centred-and-flat.*

| Principle | Essence — exemplary vs entry-level | Exemplar that does it right |
|---|---|---|
| **Rule of thirds** | Subject on a third-node, not dead-centre — tension over inertia. *Broken:* bullseye centring with no reason. | small figures on a third against vast sky (Shinkai, *Your Name*) |
| **Golden ratio / dynamic symmetry** | The figure traces a φ spiral / armature, not a grid cell. *Broken:* arbitrary placement that "just sits there". | S-curve body on the golden section (Annie Leibovitz) |
| **Leading lines** | Edges, roads, rivers, gestures route the eye to the focus. *Broken:* no path in; the eye has nowhere to go. | architecture funnels to the citadel (Dylan Cole, LOTR matte); river S-curve through depth (Levitan) |
| **Negative space / breathing room** | Emptiness *is* a compositional element — it gives scale and calm. *Broken:* every pixel crammed; no rest. | tiny figure dwarfed by void (Deakins, *Blade Runner 2049*) |
| **Framing (frame-within-frame)** | A doorway / window concentrates and means. *Broken:* a flat rectangle with no inner stage. | the closing-doorway frame (*The Searchers*) |
| **Focal hierarchy & focal point** | *One* dominant subject reads first; the rest supports. *Broken:* no focal point — the #1 generic-AI tell. | warm sunlit ruins anchor against a desaturated mass (Horizon environment art) |
| **Balance (symmetry vs asymmetry)** | Weight is deliberately distributed — formal axis *or* dynamic off-balance, never accidental. *Broken:* lopsided by neglect. | axial monolith (Cassandre, *Normandie* poster); asymmetric off-centre void (Hokusai) |
| **Figure-ground (the gestalt)** | Subject separates cleanly from ground by value/edge/colour. *Broken:* subject melts into background mush. | silhouette reads instantly against a smoky ground (Frazetta) |
| **Depth via overlapping planes** | Stacked fore/mid/far planes build real space. *Broken:* a flat sticker with no recession. | continuous-take layered rubble (*Children of Men*) |

## 2. Light & shadow — where the light falls

> *Light is the narrator. Award-tier light is motivated and structured; entry-level is flat, even, sourceless "AI daylight".*

| Principle | Essence — exemplary vs entry-level | Exemplar that does it right |
|---|---|---|
| **Three-point logic (key/fill/rim)** | A directional key sculpts form; fill controls shadow; rim separates from ground. *Broken:* flat frontal fill, no modelling. | soft key + gentle fill sculpting the figure (Leibovitz) |
| **Chiaroscuro & tenebrism** | Form emerges from near-black by a single raking beam — drama by value. *Broken:* grey, even, no shadow structure. | **tenebrism, the named engine — Caravaggio**; figures out of cathedral dark (Donato Giancola) |
| **Golden / blue hour** | Warm low key + long shadows (or cool dusk) carries time-of-day and mood. *Broken:* noon flatness; HDR over-baking. | alpenglow warm-vs-cool split (Bierstadt); magic-hour rim (Shinkai) |
| **Volumetric / god-rays** | Light made tangible through haze/smoke/rain. *Broken:* a glow sticker with no medium to scatter in. | smoke-shafted temple (Storaro, *Apocalypse Now*); rain doubling every source (*Blade Runner*) |
| **Motivated light sources** | Every source is *in-world* and explains itself. *Broken:* unmotivated rim/glow that no lamp could cast. | candlelight-only key (*Barry Lyndon*); neon practicals as the whole script (Doyle, *Fallen Angels*) |
| **Value structure & notan** | The image reads as a strong light/dark pattern *before* colour. *Broken:* muddy mid-tone soup, no value plan. | Zone-System tonal control (Ansel Adams); solid-black value in pure ink (Miura, *Berserk*) |
| **Contre-jour / silhouette** | Backlight collapses the subject to a shape against light. *Broken:* a flat cut-out with no rim or atmosphere. | contre-jour monstrous forms (Beksiński); tunnel-mouth silhouettes (Deakins, *Sicario*) |

## 3. Colour (art direction, beyond WCAG)

> *WCAG asks "can it be read"; art-direction asks "does it sing". Award-tier colour is a chosen script; entry-level is garish or muddy default-render.*

| Principle | Essence — exemplary vs entry-level | Exemplar that does it right |
|---|---|---|
| **Harmony schemes** | A chosen relationship — complementary / analogous / triadic / split-comp. *Broken:* random rainbow, no relationship. | saturated orange sun vs blue-grey, complementary (Monet, *Impression, Sunrise*); analogous warm field (Rothko) |
| **Temperature contrast (warm/cool)** | Warm light against cool shadow gives form and depth. *Broken:* one flat temperature everywhere. | amber key vs blue-black shadow (Storaro); warm peaks / cool foreground (Bierstadt) |
| **Limited / dominant palette** | A disciplined few hues + one accent — restraint reads as intent. *Broken:* every hue at full saturation ("clown-vomit"). | earthy palette + one hot-red accent (Frazetta) |
| **Atmospheric perspective (hue + value recession)** | Distant planes desaturate, cool, and lose contrast — depth in colour. *Broken:* far hills as saturated as near ones. | ranks of bamboo paling into haze (*Crouching Tiger*); layered skyline recession (Horizon) |
| **Colour as emotion / mood** | Hue carries feeling with no representational help. *Broken:* colour as mere fill, no register. | colour-as-content field (Rothko); teal-green fairy-tale script (*The Shape of Water*) |
| **The colour script** | A frame-wide, deliberate palette plan tying the whole image to one emotional key. *Broken:* incoherent local colour. | near-monochrome irradiated orange (*Blade Runner 2049*); desaturated steel-blue war (*Saving Private Ryan*) |

## 4. Narrative & mood — what the image *says*

> *An award image is a held breath of a story. Entry-level is a competent object that means nothing.*

| Principle | Essence — exemplary vs entry-level | Exemplar that does it right |
|---|---|---|
| **Implied story** | The frame implies a before/after — a whole tale at its edges. *Broken:* a static subject, no stakes. | threshold doorway implying a life of exile (*The Searchers*) |
| **The decisive moment** | Cartier-Bresson: the single instant where everything aligns. *Broken:* a limp in-between frame. | a shadow appearing to strike the ball (World Sports Photography winner); the pointing-gesture instant (Caravaggio) |
| **Gesture / line-of-action** | A figure's pose flows along one expressive arc. *Broken:* stiff, axis-less, lifeless posing. | a climber's body rising through the vertical (Red Bull Illume); a dancer's limbs into negative space (Lois Greenfield) |
| **Atmosphere** | Air, weather, haze, light-mood saturate the frame as a felt presence. *Broken:* sterile, airless render. | komorebi / breathing forest (Kazuo Oga, Ghibli) |
| **Emotional register** | The image commits to *one* feeling — dread, awe, tenderness, menace — and sustains it. *Broken:* tonally neutral, says nothing. | oppressive memento-mori (Beksiński); reverent memory-soaked interior (Lubezki, *Tree of Life*) |

## 5. Style & medium fidelity — excellent *of its kind*

> *An image must be excellent **of its declared style/genre** — a true Art Deco, not a pastiche; a believable
> golden-hour, not HDR clown-vomit; clean line-art, not muddy. Award-corpus research is organised by exactly
> these axes — **genres · styles · scenes · landscapes · diagrams · action** — and each is a fidelity yardstick.*

| Principle | Essence — exemplary vs entry-level | Exemplar that does it right |
|---|---|---|
| **Movement authenticity** | A named style obeys its *own* grammar — period palette, line-logic, surface. *Broken:* a generic-AI gloss wearing the style as a costume (pastiche). | true Art Deco geometric reduction (Cassandre); whiplash Art-Nouveau line (Mucha); Swiss grid rigour (Müller-Brockmann) |
| **Medium truth** | The image honours its medium's physics — watercolour reserves white, oil shows impasto, ink builds value by hatch density. *Broken:* a flat digital fake of the medium. | reserved-white wash (Beatrix Potter); impasto in the lights (Rembrandt); cross-hatch tonal build (editorial ink) |
| **Genre believability** | The scene reads convincingly *as its genre* — a real golden-hour landscape, a lived-in tavern, a photoreal viz. *Broken:* the tell-tale over-rendered "AI sheen". | photographic-not-CG archviz (CGarchitect winner); a lived-in crowded cantina; convincing material response (Pixar) |
| **Constraint as discipline** | Hard limits (low-poly facets, sprite grid, panel grid) are *the* aesthetic when owned, not fought. *Broken:* fighting the constraint into mush. | deliberate faceting (Timothy J. Reynolds); pixel-grid sprite discipline (*Octopath Traveler*); nine-panel grid (*Watchmen*) |

## 6. The award bar — award-tier vs competent-but-generated

> *The line our first generated heroes fell short of. Award-tier is publication/gallery-ready: intentional in
> every pixel, with a name for every choice. Competent-but-generated is the entry-level trap — polished
> surface, empty craft.*

**Award-tier** clears all of §1–§5 *and* survives §7: one clear focal point, motivated structured light, a
disciplined colour script, a felt mood, true to its style, zero artifacts. Every choice is *nameable*.

**The entry-level trap** — the named tells of competent-but-generated:

| Tell | What it is | The fix it lacks (§) |
|---|---|---|
| **No focal point** | the eye wanders; nothing reads first | focal hierarchy §1 |
| **Flat lighting** | even, frontal, sourceless "AI daylight" | three-point / motivated light §2 |
| **Muddy or garish colour** | mid-tone soup *or* every-hue-maxed clown-vomit | limited palette / colour script §3 |
| **Cliché composition** | dead-centre bullseye, no leading line, no negative space | composition §1 |
| **Baked text / anatomy artifacts** | gibberish glyphs, melted hands — see §7 | artifact floor §7 |
| **Generic "AI sheen"** | over-rendered plastic gloss; style worn as costume, not obeyed | medium truth / movement authenticity §5 |

## 7. The artifact floor — hard fails that cap any image

> *Below this line, polish is irrelevant. These align with the **Artifact-freedom** hard caps an image
> reviewer enforces (e.g. PRESSROOM's image rubric). A single instance caps the score regardless of every
> other virtue.*

| Hard fail | What disqualifies | Cap |
|---|---|---|
| **Mangled anatomy** | extra/missing limbs, fused faces, melted hands where figures are central | caps the cell **≤ 2** |
| **Gibberish text** | baked pseudo-glyphs where legible text/labels were invited | route away from this model; note explicitly |
| **Melted geometry** | duplicated/fused/dissolved forms, incoherent hard-surface | Artifact-freedom **0–1** |
| **Broken perspective** | impossible vanishing, warped planes, non-Euclidean architecture | Artifact-freedom **0–1** |

> Clearing this floor is the *precondition* of award-tier, before any named virtue earns a place.

> **The lineage these principles name:** Cartier-Bresson (the decisive moment), Caravaggio (tenebrism), the
> φ / dynamic-symmetry and rule-of-thirds traditions, atmospheric perspective, the colour-script and notan
> traditions, Cleveland–McGill/Tufte for the diagram axis. When you cite a finding, name the principle *and*
> a concrete exemplar — *"flat lighting — cf. Leibovitz's three-point key"* — so the finding is teachable and
> the designer↔reviewer loop can verify the fix. This canon is the AESTHETICS lens of the
> [design-fitness rubric](../protocols/design-critique-loop.md).
