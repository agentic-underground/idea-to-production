# Generation veins — casting a wide net for coined names

The job of generation is **breadth then distinctiveness**: produce many candidates from genuinely different
sources so the survivors aren't all minor variations of one idea. Run the veins **in parallel, one agent
per vein** (never one per name). Each vein returns candidates with a syllable count and a one-line rationale
tying the name to the charter's values.

Honour the charter's constraints throughout: the stated **values to evoke**, the **syllable target**, the
**banned/saturated stems**, and any language/tone preference. Coined (invented) words only — not dictionary
words, which are almost always taken or trademarked in the test/verify/security space.

## Source veins (mine several; don't stop at English)

1. **Latin / Greek roots** — truncate and *fuse* roots for the charter's concepts (trust, clear, proof,
   light, threshold, vouch, attest); invent, don't concatenate. (*fides→fidavel, clarus→clarivo*.)
2. **Romance** (Italian/Spanish/French/Portuguese) — warm, vowel-rich phonetics; diminutives and agentive
   suffixes (-ello, -ina, -aje, -eur).
3. **Norse / Old English / Germanic** — hard consonants, gatekeeping/proof roots (*ward, wit, lore, rune*),
   compounded and then abstracted away from the literal word.
4. **Japanese / Korean phoneme textures** — open syllables (ka, ki, ru, na, vel), modern and culturally
   neutral; good for community/openness register.
5. **Arabic / Semitic roots** — trust/contract/safety roots (*amn, wafa, haqq*); highly distinctive, near-
   zero npm/brand footprint.
6. **Pure phonetic invention** — Snyk/Tessl/Zylo-style: short, punchy, no literal meaning, just feels
   trustworthy-and-modern. Strong for ownability.

## Era / tone veins

- **Ancient / classical** — gravitas, institution, permanence.
- **Retro / mid-century** — friendly, established, human.
- **Modern startup** — clipped, lowercase, frictionless.
- **Futurist** — coined, sleek, slightly unfamiliar.

Pick eras that fit the charter tone; a security tool wants gravitas + trust, a community tool wants warmth.

## Coinage techniques (apply across the source/era veins)

- **Portmanteau / blend** — fuse two charged morphemes at a shared sound (*verify+vault→vervault*).
- **Clipping** — cut a longer word to an ownable stub (*attestation→attesa*).
- **Affixation** — add an agentive/quality suffix (-el, -is, -um, -ia, -vel, -or, -yx) to a root stub.
- **Phonosemantic coinage** — choose sounds whose *texture* signals the value (hard stops = security; soft
  liquids = trust/calm), independent of literal meaning.
- **Reduplication / rhythm** — memorable cadence (CVC-CVC), good for sayability.

## Quality bar per candidate

Easy to say and spell on first hearing · no unfortunate meaning/slang in a major language (flag for the
challenge phase, don't self-clear) · encodes at least one charter value traceably · within the syllable
target · avoids every banned stem. Generate generously (aim 40–60 deduped); the deterministic verify and
the adversarial challenge will do the culling.
