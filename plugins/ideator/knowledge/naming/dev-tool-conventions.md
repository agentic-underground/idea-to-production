# Developer-tool naming — power-adjacency & intellectual humour

Naming for a technical/developer audience has its own register. Two veins live here: **borrowed
equity** (power-adjacency) and **intellectual humour** (the insider wink). Both reward an audience
that values a classical education signal or a clever in-joke.

## Power-adjacency / borrowed equity

Borrow the connotation of a powerful concept — myth, science, Latin — **without** infringing a mark.
The name inherits gravitas the product hasn't yet earned, and the metaphor teaches the function.

- **Mythology.** *Kubernetes* (Greek κυβερνήτης, "helmsman" — it steers containers like a helmsman
  steers a ship; abbreviated **k8s**, a numeronym). *Prometheus* (Titan who brought fire → brings
  *visibility*; a monitoring tool). *Hermes* (messenger → APIs/transport). *Atlas, Apollo, Janus,
  Pandora* — each a named figure whose story *is* the positioning.
- **Science / astronomy / Latin.** Elements, constellations (*Cygnus, Apus, Grus, Pavo*), genera
  (*Otus* the owl), physics terms. Real-but-obscure → built-in imagery + a clean namespace.
- **How it works:** choose a figure/term whose canonical *trait* matches the product's core value;
  the audience does the inference and feels clever for it. Verify the borrowed word isn't itself a
  live mark in the category (e.g. *Prometheus*, *Hermes* are crowded).

## Intellectual humour / the insider wink

Dev culture prizes self-aware, witty names. Done well, the joke *is* the memorability.

- *git* — Torvalds: "I name all my projects after myself" (British slang "git" = idiot); self-
  deprecating hacker ethos, three letters, trivially typed.
- *Caddy* — a helper that carries the tedious load (HTTPS/config) for you.
- *Vim* — "Vi IMproved" (a backronym that also means *energy/vigour*).
- *Docker* — shipping-container metaphor made literal.
- The wink works only for the right audience (a SCRATCH "curse-of-knowledge" risk for a mass market —
  gate it on the discovery brief's humour appetite).

## Dev-audience conventions

- **Lowercase** throughout — signals open-source, modern, non-corporate (github, docker, k8s, caddy).
- **Short & terse** — CLI-friendly, fast to type; 1–2 syllables; often abbreviates (k8s, i18n, a11y).
- **Numeronyms** — compress long terms into in-group shorthand (k8s, l10n, i18n, a11y).
- **Metaphor with functional clarity** — the name teaches the tool (Docker, Terraform, Caddy).
- **Compound/portmanteau** of functional concepts (GitHub, GitLab).
- **Mythology/classical** for credibility with a technical, educated audience.

## Discipline

- Power-adjacency and humour veins are **opt-in via the discovery brief** (power-adjacency targets;
  humour appetite none ↔ subtle ↔ overt). Don't force a pun on a Ruler/Sage enterprise brand, or
  gravitas-myth on a Jester product.
- Famous myth/science words are heavily taken — pair with the checker's neighbour + registry pass and
  prefer the obscure end (genera, minor constellations) for a clean namespace.
