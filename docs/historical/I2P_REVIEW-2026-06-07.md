## Verdict rationale

**NEEDS_REVISION.** No surviving **CRITICAL**: the most dramatic finding — that the entire post-launch
operating life of a product is untooled — is real but **deliberately and honestly scoped out** in
`product-lifecycle.md:18-19` ("downstream / out of scope … acknowledged, not tooled"), so it is a strategic
limitation and a name-vs-scope mismatch, not a hidden defect. What pushes the verdict past PASS is a cluster of
unresolved **HIGH** defects and gaps: a **front-door doc that contradicts itself** about the phase order, a
**marketplace-wide phase-ordering + naming split** (`ASSURE` vs `SECURE`, `BUILD▸DESIGN` vs `DESIGN→BUILD`), the
**foundry reviewer agent missing its own adversarial machinery** (the refute-stance, severity calibration, and
refutation pass live only in the `pr-review` *orchestrator*, so any other caller gets a rubber-stampable
checklist), **no independent reviewer at DISCOVER/IDEATE** (producer self-challenge only), and **whole reviewer
lenses absent** (API-contract, observability, licensing, prompt-injection, i18n, document-a11y).

## Answers to the three questions asked

**1. Are the adversarial reviewers advanced / specialised / knowledgeable enough?** — *Two-tier.*
- **Exemplary:** atelier's `ui-design-reviewer` and pressroom's design reviewers are genuinely SOTA — named
  canon by identifier (WCAG 2.2 by SC number, Gestalt, Nielsen, Norman, Bringhurst measure/leading,
  Cleveland–McGill, Tufte lie-factor, ColorBrewer), weighted 0–5 rubrics, **hard gates** (a11y, lie-factor),
  bounded convergent loops, evidence-attached findings. These are the template.
- **Strong-but-structurally-weak:** foundry's `reviewer` agent is a concrete, domain-specific *checklist*
  (EARS/BDD/SMU/coverage roles are real), **but its adversarial stance, severity rubric, and refutation pass are
  not in the agent** — confirmed: `grep -niE "refute|adversar|severity|false.positiv"` on `reviewer.md` returns
  effectively nothing. They live in `pr-review/SKILL.md`, where the refutation pass is even marked **(Optional)**.
  Its `SECURITY-REVIEWER` is a weak eyeball OWASP pass with no scanner, no CWE/OWASP IDs, and no deferral to
  SENTINEL at the agent level.
- **Real-tool-backed but mis-tiered:** SENTINEL runs real scanners (gitleaks, Semgrep MCP, SCA) and degrades
  honestly, but its *authoritative* `secret-scan` runs on **haiku** while the reviewer policy demands the
  strongest tier for the lens others trust.
- **Not reviewed at all:** DISCOVER and IDEATE have **no independent reviewer** — only the producing agent
  challenging itself (confirmed: `ideator/agents/` and `market-scanner/agents/` contain only `inspector.md`).
  Self-grading is the exact failure mode the panel exists to prevent.

**2. Does it cover the product lifecycle adequately?** — *Excellent creation arc; the operating half is absent.*
It covers idea → first live deploy beautifully (6 staffed phases). But everything *after* go-live —
observability, incident response, iteration/analytics, maintenance, deprecation — has **zero tooling**. This is
disclosed (`IN PRODUCTION` owner = `—`), so it is a deliberate boundary; the problem is the name **idea-to-
PRODUCTION** promises living-in-production while the scope stops at the moment of going live.

**3. Anything missing?** — Yes, three classes: **(a)** the post-launch operating half of the lifecycle;
**(b)** whole reviewer lenses (API/semver, observability, licensing, prompt-injection/LLM-safety, i18n, document
accessibility); **(c)** independent reviewers at the front end. Plus the concrete drift defects below.

## Per-lens verdicts

- **REVIEWER-DEPTH — NEEDS_REVISION.** Design/print reviewers exemplary; foundry code-reviewer's adversarial
  machinery is in the wrong layer and its security role is weak; front-end stages have no independent reviewer;
  several whole lenses missing.
- **LIFECYCLE-COVERAGE — NEEDS_REVISION.** Creation arc fully staffed; the entire post-launch operating half is
  untooled (disclosed, so a scope/name decision — not a defect, but the headline gap).
- **INTEGRITY/DRIFT — NEEDS_REVISION.** Canonical copies, manifests, and hooks are **clean** (md5-verified
  SOUL/inject-soul/check/inspection-core; gatehouse→concierge rename fully complete). Four concrete defects: the
  self-contradicting help doc, the marketplace-wide ordering/naming split, a dangling sentinel link, and the
  self-gamed scorecard metric.

## What was NOT reviewed (honest gaps)

- **The four runtime lenses** (CODE / DESIGN / DOCS / SECURITY) ran no specialist scanner — the tree is clean,
  so foundry `/pr-review`, atelier `/ui-review`, pressroom `/pressroom:design-review`, and sentinel
  `/security-gate` had no change to bite. This was a **design audit**, not a diff review.
- **No runtime behaviour was exercised** — hooks, `lifecycle.sh`, and `cost.sh` were read, not executed end-to-end.
- **Reviewer findings are from reading the prompts**, not from observing the reviewers run on a real PR — a
  reviewer that reads well can still miss in practice; the inverse is also possible.
- **The exemplary-tier judgement** (atelier/pressroom) is based on the canon they cite, not on measured outcomes
  against real users.
